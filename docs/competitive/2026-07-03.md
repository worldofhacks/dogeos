   current reality. **Actionable — propose issue:** "Cross-chain phase-1:
   refresh NEAR-Intents spec vs KyberCross OneClick + ERC-7683".
   ([LeoDex](https://leodex.io/learn/near-intents/how-to-swap-near-intents),
   [eco.com](https://eco.com/support/en/articles/13064541-what-is-kyberswap-2026-guide))
2. **Relay bridges 85+ chains and is "designed to add chains on Day 1."**
   No Dogecoin/DogeOS support found today, but they are the most likely first
   external bridge/aggregator on DogeOS mainnet. Response: monitor their
   chains page weekly; our counter is first-party router + deepest MuchFi/
   Barkswap coverage. ([relay.link](https://relay.link/),
   [docs](https://docs.relay.link/solutions/chains))
3. **ERC-7683 (Uniswap × Across) standardizes cross-chain intents.** If DogeOS
   canonical bridge tooling adopts it, generic fillers commoditize routing.

## Incidents

- **SwapNet aggregator, $16.8M — authorization bypass draining pre-approved
  funds** via a logic flaw in approval handling. *Applies to us?* This is our
  #1 designed-against class: ingress only via canonical Permit2, immutable
  venue whitelist, calldata allowlist. **But** the live prod router
  (`0xa315...a35A`) is the pre-hardening build lacking H1–H11, still
  EOA-owned, timelock handover unexecuted (per
  `.claude/skills/trail-of-bits-security/SKILL.md`). This incident is the
  argument for executing the redeploy runbook *before* mainnet stakes exist.
  **Actionable — propose issue:** "Execute REDEPLOY-RUNBOOK: cut over to
  hardened router + timelock ownership".
  ([cryptorank](https://cryptorank.io/news/feed/2330a-matcha-meta-dex-exploit-swapnet))
- **CoW Swap, $1.2M — domain hijack (April 14).** *Applies?* Yes, but to web
  infra, not contracts: DNS/registrar compromise serving malicious calldata.
  Mitigation belongs on the mainnet checklist: registrar lock, DNSSEC, CSP,
  bytecode/address pinning in the UI.
  ([altfins](https://altfins.com/blog/defi-hacks-2026/))
- **June 14 bridge exploit, $127M — validators signed on inclusion, not
  finality.** *Applies?* Not to phase-0 (read-only, canonical portal only —
  keep it that way). Phase-1 rule: any bridge integration must be
  finality-aware. ([nadcab](https://www.nadcab.com/blog/defi-bridge-exploit-june-cross-chain))
- **Kelp DAO $292M (LayerZero bridge) and Drift $285M (social engineering);
  ~76% of 2026 losses attributed to state-backed actors.** *Applies?* The
  common thread is ops/key compromise, not code — reinforces the timelock
  handover above and least-privilege on deploy keys.
  ([coindesk](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/04/19/the-usd292-million-kelp-exploit-how-it-happened-and-what-it-means-for-defi),
  [ccn](https://www.ccn.com/education/crypto/defi-hacks-exploits-causes-crypto-stolen-2026/))
